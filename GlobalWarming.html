What is Global Warming?

Global warming refers to the long-term rise in Earth's average surface temperature due to human activities, primarily the emission of greenhouse gases. Since the Industrial Revolution, the planet's temperature has increased by approximately 1.1°C, with the last decade being the warmest on record. This warming is causing widespread changes to our climate system, including rising sea levels, melting ice caps, more frequent extreme weather events, and disruptions to ecosystems. The scientific consensus is clear: human activities, especially burning fossil fuels and deforestation, are the dominant cause of the observed warming since the mid-20th century. Without significant reductions in emissions, global temperatures are projected to rise by 2.5°C to 4.5°C by 2100, with catastrophic consequences for life on Earth.

Major Causes of Global Warming

  • Burning of Fossil Fuels: Coal, oil, and natural gas combustion releases large amounts of CO₂.
  • Deforestation: Trees absorb CO₂; cutting them down increases greenhouse gases.
  • Industrial Processes: Factories emit various greenhouse gases like methane and nitrous oxide.
  • Agriculture: Livestock produce methane, and fertilizer use releases nitrous oxide.
  • Waste Management: Decomposition of waste in landfills produces methane.
  • Transportation: Cars, trucks, and airplanes emit CO₂ and other pollutants.

    How to Reduce Global Warming

    🌲

    Plant more trees and support reforestation efforts to absorb CO₂.

    🚶‍♂️

    Use public transport, carpool, bike, or walk to reduce vehicle emissions.

    💡

    Switch to renewable energy sources like solar and wind power.

    ♻️

    Reduce, reuse, and recycle to minimize waste and energy use.

    🥕

    Adopt sustainable eating habits by reducing meat consumption.

    🏠

    Improve home insulation and energy efficiency to lower heating/cooling needs.
